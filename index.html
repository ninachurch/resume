Co-Founder and Spokesperson

Nika Water - 2009 - 2013

After a family trip to Ethiopia and Kenya, I felt powerless. It didnâ€™t make sense in my 14-year-old mind that such disparity could exist between myself and those I'd met. My family and I decided to find a sustainable way to keep girls in developing countries out of early marriages and in school. That was the beginning of Nika Water, an eco-friendly bottled water company born of research, support, and above all love. Nika donated 100% of its profits towards poverty alleviation projects around the world and in it's 4 years gained national distribution and helped bring clean water to over 28,000 people for the rest of their lives.

Responsibilities: As a family startup, we did whatever was needed. Below are just a few of my highlighted roles.

  1. Helped design the bottle, label, name and tagline of the brand.
  2. worked street fairs to perform customer development
  3. Spoke at over 60 events, conferences and workshops running programs on how youth can 'find their passions' and how the world can greater leverage social entrepreneurship.

Key Learnings

  1. You do what you have to do - if you're the CEO and there's no one else there to drive the truck, you drive it. You have to play smart, but you have to work tirelessly to make a dream come true.
  2. There are A LOT of water companies - Nika served an amazing role in my family's life. It was ultimately unsustainable because of the highly saturated space.
  3. Social Entrepreneurship is the future - If our consumer society can engrain social business into our foundation, we can revolutionize the way our society does good.

facilitator

Me to We Trips - July 2013 - September 2013

After my involvement in Nika, I worked with one of our key partner organizations helping to faciliate leadership development service trips in Kenya and Ghana for the summer.

Responsibilities:

  3. ran leadership activities and team builders
